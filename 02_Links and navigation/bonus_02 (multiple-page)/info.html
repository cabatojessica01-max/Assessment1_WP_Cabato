Key Information

"Bones" is an American crime procedural drama television series that combines forensic science with FBI investigations. The show is based loosely on the real-life experiences and novels of forensic anthropologist Kathy Reichs.

The series ran for 12 seasons and became one of the longest-running crime dramas on television. It focuses on solving murders through scientific examination of human remains, often highlighting the importance of forensic anthropology in modern criminal justice.

The setting primarily takes place at the Jeffersonian Institute in Washington, D.C., and various crime scenes investigated by the FBI. The show blends scientific accuracy with fictional storytelling to create engaging and educational content.

Each episode typically features a “case-of-the-week” structure, while also building long-term character development and overarching storylines across seasons.

The series is known for combining elements of crime, drama, humor, and emotional storytelling, making it appealing to a wide audience beyond just crime show fans.
